## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

### Key Shots That Make This Work
1. **The 3-panel conflict reveal (Frame 2A):** Most critical visual. Must be immediately clear there's a mismatch.
2. **The manager's face (Frames 2B, 3B):** Authenticity is everything. Real frustration/defeat sells the pain point.
3. **The black screen pause (Frame 4A):** Only 0.2s but creates dramatic impact. Don't skip.
4. **The logo reveal (Frame 4B):** Must feel heroic and powerful. SFX + music + animation alignment is crucial.
5. **The dashboard reveal (Frame 4C):** Shows actual product. Make it look premium and intuitive.

### Emotional Beats to Nail
- **Confusion (0-3s):** Viewer should feel confused by conflicting data
- **Panic (3-6s):** Viewer should feel urgency of the problem
- **Defeat (6-9s):** Viewer should empathize with manager's pain
- **Relief (9-12s):** Viewer should feel the "hero saves the day" moment
- **Inspiration (12-15s):** Viewer should want the solution (download guide)

### Technical Priorities
1. **Audio:** Sound design is 50% of impact. Invest here.
2. **Pacing:** Each 3-second beat must grip attention. No dead moments.
3. **Color grading:** Show shift from tense/cold to hopeful/warm.
4. **Continuity:** Same actor throughout for emotional connection.

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Overly cheesy superhero tropes (keep it grounded, parody)
- ❌ Confusing dashboard (simplify, only show key metrics)
- ❌ Bad actor performance (budget for good talent or multiple takes)
- ❌ Unclear CTA (make link very obvious, big text)
- ❌ Slow pacing (keep energy high, especially first 9 seconds)
- ❌ Mismatched audio/visuals (sync music, VO, SFX perfectly)
